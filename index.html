PandaWare

EMG-controlled myoelectric prosthetic hand, built from scratch.

Read the Paper ↗ Read the Poster ↗ Documentation Log ↗

Tech Stack

RP2040 MicroPython MyoWare 2.0 PCA9685 INA219 MG996R servos tendon-driven actuation

Overview

  • Full mechanical design and fabrication across multiple 3D-printed iterations, refining geometry for fit, range of motion, and durability.
  • Custom circuit design and assembly—integrating servo control, EMG acquisition, and power sensing on a single system.
  • Custom EMG signal pipeline with adaptive normalization, enabling reliable grip detection across varying muscle fatigue and electrode placement.
  • Closed-loop current sensing via INA219 for grip force limiting, preventing damage to objects and improving safety.

[ Tech Stack ]

Every component chosen for a reason. Here's what powers PandaWare.

[ 01 ] Microcontroller

RP2040

Dual-core ARM Cortex-M0+ at 133 MHz. Main compute unit controlling servo PWM output, I²C sensor bus, and real-time EMG acquisition loop.

[ 02 ] Firmware

MicroPython

Python-based firmware running on the RP2040. Handles the full signal processing pipeline, servo control loop, and I²C bus coordination.

[ 03 ] EMG Sensor

MyoWare 2.0

Surface electromyography sensor capturing forearm muscle activation. Outputs an analog voltage representing muscle contraction intensity for grip detection.

[ 04 ] PWM Driver

PCA9685

16-channel PWM/servo driver over I²C. Generates precise servo signals for all 5 finger motors, offloading timing-critical work from the main CPU.

[ 05 ] Current Sensor

INA219

High-side DC current and power sensor over I²C. Monitors servo current draw in real time for closed-loop grip force limiting and object safety.

[ 06 ] Actuators

MG996R Servos

High-torque metal gear servos, one per finger. Drive tendon cables through 3D-printed finger channels for independent, human-like digit actuation.

[ 07 ] Fabrication

FDM 3D Printing

Fused deposition modeling in PLA+. Multiple design iterations per finger joint to refine range of motion, tendon routing, and structural durability.

[ 08 ] Mechanism

Tendon Actuation

Stainless steel cables routed through finger channels, anchored at fingertip and servo horn. Lightweight, human-analogous flexion with minimal moving parts.
