PandaWare

EMG-controlled myoelectric prosthetic hand, built from scratch

Tech stack

  • RP2040
  • MicroPython
  • MyoWare 2.0
  • PCA9685
  • INA219
  • MG996R servos
  • Tendon-driven actuation

PandaWare is a fully self-built myoelectric hand. I handled the entire mechanical design and fabrication, working through multiple 3D-printed iterations to refine the tendon-driven actuation and the fit of each finger before settling on the final geometry.

On the electronics side, I designed and assembled the circuit from the ground up and wrote a custom EMG signal pipeline with adaptive normalization, so the hand stays responsive as muscle signals drift during use. A closed-loop current-sensing layer reads motor draw in real time to limit grip force — letting the hand hold delicate objects without crushing them.
